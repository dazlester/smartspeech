Does My Child Need a Speech Assessment?

If you or someone else notices that your child is struggling with their speech, it is important that you take the first step of having them assessed by a Speech and Language Therapist.

The therapist can let you know if your child has a difficulty and most importantly, what to do if this is the case.

Here at SmartSpeech, we specialise in assessing children who have speech and language difficulties, and thanks to our modern approach to therapy, we can typically provide an assessment, with advice and activities to help the child progress, within 24hrs.

A child with speech and language difficulties can become frustrated over time and lose confidence in their talking. Speech is so important, and if a child can’t make themselves understood, it can impact their friendships, behaviour and sometimes their progress at school. Reading and writing in particular, can be tricky for a child who is not yet saying the right sounds.

In many cases, with the right advice and activities to work on at home, these children can make excellent progress. That’s where SmartSpeech comes in.

So take the first step today, and fill in our free online assessment consultation questionnaire, to have your child's problems reviewed by one of our experienced speech and language therapists.

Examples of problems your child might be experiencing which we can help with:

- Mixing up sounds like k’ and ‘t’ (e.g. “tite” for ‘kite’) or ‘f’ and ‘b’ (e.g. “bour” for ‘four’);
- Missing off sounds from the ends of words (e.g. “ca” for ‘cat’);
- Leaves sounds off the beginning of words (eg. "three" is "ee" and "boy" is "oy";
- Simplifies combinations like 'sp', 'str' or 'br' (eg. "spider" is "bider" and "strawberry" is "tawbee");
- Overusing one ‘favourite’ sound (e.g. using ‘d’ at the start of everything);
- Only uses vowels (eg. "I am three" would be "I ah eee");
- Difficulty articulating specific sounds like ‘s’ or ‘sh’.
- Has difficulty using 'ch' and 'j' sounds (eg. "chair" is "tair" and "jam" is "dam");
- Repeats sounds in words (eg. "c-c-c-cat");
- Stretches sounds out (eg. "mmmmummy" or "aaaapple");
- Experiences 'blocks' where no sound will come out;
- Repeats whole words/phrases (eg. "I think, I think, I think I want juice please")

Please note: There are other specific problems which we can help with. If your child's problem is not listed above, please fill in our questionnaire and we will be able to advise you.

Any child who has enough language to get a suitable sample from can receive a speech sound assessment. Even very young children can have an assessment through informal observations of their play and talking. If your child doesn’t have many words yet, it will not be possible to assess their speech. Please feel free to fill in our questionnaire if you are unsure and our speech and language therapists can advise you further.

Video has been proven to be very effective in assessing speech. In fact, it can allow for more in-depth assessments due to the ability to re-play the recorded assessment. It also removes barriers to traditional speech assessment services for those who live in rural areas, where there are limited services available, or where you face lengthy waiting times.

SmartSpeech provides speech sound assessments only, and is not a full assessment of language and communication skills. For many children, this is the only area of need, and so the advice provided by SmartSpeech will be enough to support the development of their communication skills. However, if you have concerns about another area of your child’s communication skills (e.g. social communication, language skills or a stammer), it would be helpful to see a speech and language therapist face-to-face for a more thorough assessment. If you are unsure whether you should use SmartSpeech or not, fill in the initial questionnaire and our speech and language therapists can advise you and help you make the decision.
