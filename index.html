Does My Child Need a Speech Assessment?

If you notice that your child is struggling with their speech, it is important that you take the first step and have them assessed by a Speech and Language Therapist.

The therapist can let you know if your child has a difficulty and most importantly, what to do if this is the case.

Here at SmartSpeech, we specialise in working with children who have speech sound difficulties. These children can be hard to understand, especially to people who don’t know them well.

Some examples of errors they might make include;
- mixing up sounds like k’ and ‘t’ (e.g. “tite” for ‘kite’) or ‘f’ and ‘b’ (e.g. “bour” for ‘four’);
- missing off sounds from the ends of words (e.g. “ca” for ‘cat’);
- overusing one ‘favourite’ sound (e.g. using ‘d’ at the start of everything);
- difficulty articulating specific sounds like ‘s’ or ‘sh’.

A child with speech sound difficulties can become frustrated over time and lose confidence in their talking. Speech is so important! If a child can’t make themselves understood, it can impact their friendships, behaviour and sometimes their progress at school. Reading and writing in particular, can be tricky for a child who is not yet saying the right sounds.

In many cases, with the right advice and activities, these children can make excellent progress. That’s where SmartSpeech comes in.
